About Me

I am a Doctoral Researcher and PhD student at the University of Turku, conducting research within the Trienkatu lab. My academic work centers on autonomous systems and robotics, with a specialized focus on the holistic energy optimization of swarm mobile robots.

My research addresses the critical challenge of sustainability in electric vehicle swarms by developing integrated co-optimization frameworks. Rather than looking at components in isolation, my models account for the entire ecosystem of energy consumption—balancing mechanical propulsion dynamics, real-time computational overhead (CPU/GPU workloads), and wireless communication energy models. Furthermore, to ensure long-term operational viability, our framework explicitly integrates battery health and aging characteristics (State-of-Health uncertainty) into the fleet routing and optimization loops.

To train, validate, and scale these complex multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) models and evolutionary algorithms, I leverage advanced simulation environments including NVIDIA Isaac Sim and Isaac Lab alongside real-time hardware profiling setups.
