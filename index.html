About Me

I am a Doctoral Researcher and PhD candidate at the University of Turku, conducting research within the Autonomous Systems Laboratory (ASL) at the Trienkatu lab. My expertise lies at the intersection of multi-agent robotics, embedded systems, and evolutionary optimization.

My core research introduces holistic co-optimization frameworks for autonomous electric vehicle (EV) swarms. Rather than analyzing components in isolation, my methodology couples mechanical propulsion physics, chip-level computational workloads (DVFS CPU/GPU profiling), and wireless communication energy overhead. To ensure long-term operational viability under real-world conditions, my frameworks explicitly integrate battery health degradation and State-of-Health (SoH) uncertainty into dynamic routing, path planning, and fleet orchestration algorithms.
